Carbs intake

By Wirula

Carbohydrates provide energy to your brain, red blood cells and muscles, and account for the majority of the calories consumed in a standard diet. The daily carbohydrate requirements for women can vary depending on age, physical activity levels, health status and weight. The type of carbohydrate you choose to include in your diet can make a difference in your health. Candies, desserts, white bread, white rice and refined breakfast cereals can quickly elevate your blood sugar levels, which can put you at risk of developing Type 2 diabetes. Starch Foods high in starch include:
  • Starchy vegetables like peas, corn, lima beans and potatoes
  • Dried beans, lentils and peas
  • Grains like oats, barley and rice.
The grain group can be broken down even further into whole grain or refined grain. A grain contains three parts, bran
  • germ
  • endosperm
The bran is the outer hard shell of the grain. It is the part of the grain that provides the most fiberThe part of food that is hard to digest. Foods high in fiber take longer to digest and therefore affect your blood glucose more slowly (i.e. whole wheat bread, prunes and other vegetables)X and most of the B vitamins and minerals. The germ is the next layer and is packed with nutrients including essential fatty acids and vitamin E. The endosperm is the soft part in the center of the grain. It contains the starch If you eat a whole grain food, it contains the bran, germ, and endosperm so you get all of the nutrients that whole grains have to offer. Whole grain means that the entire grain kernel is in the food. If you eat a refined grain food, it contains only the endosperm or the starchy part so you miss out on a lot of vitamins and minerals. Because whole grains contain the entire grain, they are much more nutritious than refined grains. Sugar Can be:
  • naturally occurring sugars such as those in milk or fruit
  • added sugars such as those added during processing such as fruit canned in heavy syrup or sugar added to make a cookie


On the nutrition facts label, the number of sugar grams includes both added and natural sugars. There are many different names for sugar. Examples of common names are table sugar, brown sugar, molasses, honey, beet sugar, cane sugar, confectioner's sugar, powdered sugar, maple syrup, high-fructose corn syrup, agave nectar and sugar cane syrup. Table sugar is also known as sucrosea two-part sugar made of glucose and fructose. Known as table sugar or white sugar, it is found naturally in sugar cane and in beets.X. Fruit sugar is also known as fructose and the sugar in milk is called lactose. You can recognize other sugars on labels because their chemical names also end in "-ose." For example glucose (also called dextrose), fructose (also called levulose), lactose and maltose.

Fiber Fiber comes from plant foods so there is no fiber in animal products such as milk, eggs, meat, poultry, and fish. It is the part of plant foods that is edible, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts and legumes. When you consume dietary fiber, most of it passes through the intestines and is not digested. Make sure that at least half of the grains you eat are whole grains because you won’t get any fiber unless you eat the whole grain. The best sources of fiber are beans, while some of the top choices for soluble fiber include bran, oats, beans, broccoli, brussels sprouts, carrots, green peas, apples, oranges and pears. Fiber contributes to digestive health, helps to keep you regular, and helps to make you feel full and satisfied after eating.
